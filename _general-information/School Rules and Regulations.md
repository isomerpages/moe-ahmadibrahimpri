<p><strong><u>School Rules and Regulations</u></strong>
</p>
<p>THE PRESCRIBED UNIFORM</p>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>You must wear the full uniform / school attire, at school or on outings
(including Saturdays, school holidays and camps in school).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Name tags are to be worn at all times.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>Boys: Name tags to be placed on the upper edge of the pocket.</p>
<p>Girls: Name tags to be placed below the school crest.</p>
<p>The pupils’ name tags are colour-coded by cohort. Please ensure that the
name tags are ironed on / sewn on&nbsp;<strong>below the school badge</strong>.</p>

<p><strong><u>Anti-Bullying Policy</u></strong>
</p>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Bullying refers to deliberate and repeated behaviour that causes physical,
emotional, or psychological harm. It may occur:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>on school premises,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>during school-related activities (including online platforms) or</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>outside school when it affects students’ wellbeing or the school environment.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>AIPS maintains a <strong>zero-tolerance approach</strong> towards bullying.
Every member of our school community deserves to feel safe, respected,
and cared for. No one should be bullied, and no one should engage in bullying
behaviour.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Reporting Procedures</strong>
</p>
<p>Students and parents may report bullying concerns directly to teachers,
school counsellors, or school leaders. All reports will be taken seriously
and handled sensitively to safeguard our students’ wellbeing.</p>

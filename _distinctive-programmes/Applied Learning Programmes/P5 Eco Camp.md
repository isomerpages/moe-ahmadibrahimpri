<p>The Primary 5 students went for a trip to the NEWater Visitor Centre in
term 1. The NEWater Visitor Centre (NVC) is an education hub that promotes
water sustainability in Singapore and shares how NEWater is produced. With
interactive displays and authentic equipment used, our students had a first-hands
look at the innerworkings of the entire process.</p>
<p>The objectives of the learning journey are:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>To give our students insights on the national taps of Singapore and the
journey taken by the Public Utilities Board in supporting the needs of
Singapore with fresh drinkable water.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To be familiar with filtration process to get NEWater.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To witness the authentic equipment used in the NEWater process.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<p>The Primary 5 students went to the Lower Seletar Reservoir and learnt
about the history and design features of the reservoir. They also participated
in some activities such as the global warming game and litter picking.
Through this eco-camp, the students learn and appreciate the water conservation
efforts in Singapore.</p>
<p>The objectives of this Eco-Camp are to:</p>
<p>• Appreciate the importance of having clean water resources in Singapore</p>
<p>• Understand how they can contribute towards water sustainability in Singapore</p>
